Laura García-Montoya

Book Manuscript

My book project uncovers largely overlooked variation in long-term patterns of inequality within Latin America and explains how and why some countries managed to escape inequality traps while others did not. Using a combination of qualitative and quantitative methodologies, I Inequality traps in Latin America can be explained by the persistence of state-elite equilibriums, shaped by paradigms of progress, and embedded institutionally during the period of state formation. When state-elite equilibriums are disrupted, countries can overcome inequality traps. Through in-depth comparative analysis of a small number of cases, I offer a historically grounded account of long-term trajectories of inequality and highlights the overlooked importance of ideas and narratives of progress in shaping redistributive institutions. In particular, I explore the process through which redistributive institutions are created and transformed. In so doing, my project demonstrates the deeply political nature of economic inequality.
