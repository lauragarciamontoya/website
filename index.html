Laura García Montoya

I am a PhD Candidate in Political Science at Northwestern University. “Trapped by Inequality: The Politics of Redistribution in Latin America” in June 2020. While doing my PhD, I also completed a MS degree in Statistics at Northwestern. I also have MA and BA degrees in Economics from the University of Los Andes in Colombia. My research and teaching interests are in the fields of comparative politics and methodology. Substantively, I focus on the political economic of inequality and political violence in Latin America. As a methodologist, I am interested in how to leverage multi-method tools to strengthen research designs and causal inference. My dissertation: "Trapped by Inequality: The Politics of Redistribution in Latin America" identifies economic inequality traps and explains how and why some countries manage to escape them and arrive at paths of diminishing inequality.
