I am a PhD Candidate in Political Science at Northwestern University. My research interests lie in the fields of comparative politics and research methodology. Within the field of comparative politics, my work focuses on the political economy of inequality and violence with a regional focus in Latin America. As a methodologist, I am interested in how to leverage multi-method tools to strengthen research designs and causal inference. My dissertation identifies economic inequality traps and explains how and why some countries manage to escape them and arrive at paths of diminishing inequality.
